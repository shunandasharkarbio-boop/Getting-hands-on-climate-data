# 🌍 What is the Climate in Paris?

To better understand the **past and present climate** of Paris, we begin by examining **monthly average temperatures**. Analyzing long-term monthly averages helps identify seasonal temperature patterns and provides a clearer picture of the city's climate.

---

# 🌸 Seasonality

## 🧪 Hands-on Exercise: Monthly Climatological Temperature in Paris

### 🎯 Objective

Calculate the **average temperature for each month** using daily temperature observations from **1950 to 2019**.

A period of more than **30 years** is considered sufficient for calculating **climatological averages**, making this dataset appropriate for climate analysis.

---

## ❓ Questions

1. Which is the **warmest summer month** in Paris (June, July, or August)?
2. Which is the **coolest winter month** in Paris (December, January, or February)?

---

## 📊 Results

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| 🌞 Warmest summer month (JJA) | **July** — **19.92 °C** |
| ❄️ Coolest winter month (DJF) | **January** — **4.47 °C** |

> **Observation:**  
> July recorded the highest average summer temperature (**19.92 °C**). However, the difference between **July** and **August** is very small, indicating similarly warm conditions during both months.

---

## 🔬 Methodology

To calculate monthly climatological temperatures, the **date column** in the dataset was first separated into three individual components:

| Original Format | Extracted Columns |
|-----------------|------------------|
| `YYYY-MM-DD` | **Year** • **Month** • **Day** |

This transformation makes it possible to:

- 📅 Group records by month
- 📊 Calculate average monthly temperatures
- 🌍 Analyze seasonal climate patterns
- 📈 Compare temperatures across multiple decades
